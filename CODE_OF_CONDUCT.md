# Code of Conduct

## Our pledge

We pledge to make participation in PatchScope a harassment-free experience for everyone,
regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and
expression, experience level, education, socioeconomic status, nationality, personal appearance,
race, caste, color, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We will act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and
healthy community.

## Expected behavior

- Show empathy and respect for different viewpoints and experiences.
- Give and accept constructive, specific feedback.
- Focus criticism on ideas and changes, not people.
- Respect privacy and avoid sharing another person's private information.
- Take responsibility, apologize when needed, and learn from mistakes.

## Unacceptable behavior

- Sexualized language or imagery, unwanted attention, or advances;
- trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks;
- public or private harassment, threats, or intimidation;
- publishing another person's private information without explicit permission;
- conduct that would reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies in project spaces and when someone is officially representing the
project in public spaces. Project spaces include issues, pull requests, reviews, discussions, and
other collaboration channels operated by PatchScope maintainers.

## Reporting and enforcement

Report public, non-sensitive moderation concerns to a maintainer in the relevant thread. For a
confidential report, contact the repository owner through their GitHub profile and ask for a private
moderation channel without putting the report details in that first message. Do not use a public
issue or a security advisory for a conduct report, and do not include unrelated source code or
credentials.

Maintainers will review reports fairly and privately, may remove or edit contributions and comments,
and may temporarily or permanently restrict participation when behavior is inappropriate,
threatening, harmful, or disruptive. Retaliation against a reporter is itself a violation.
